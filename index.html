About

I develop fast, scalable computational methods for earthquake risk and probabilistic seismic hazard analysis. My recent work uses adaptive importance sampling, linearized ground-motion–fragility coupling, and probabilistic PCA to quantify seismic hazard and its uncertainties for critical infrastructure. Before Berkeley, I spent six years as a seismic hazard analyst and seismologist in Korea, with earlier training in seismology and earth system sciences.

Research Interests

  • Earthquake risk modeling for infrastructure
  • Importance Sampling and Uncertainty Quantification
  • AI and ML methods for hazard and risk
  • Probabilistic seismic hazard analysis (PSHA)
  • Engineering seismology and ground motion modeling
